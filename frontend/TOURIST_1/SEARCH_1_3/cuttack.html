Cuttack

Known as the "Millennium City" and "Silver City," Cuttack is one of Odisha's oldest cities, serving as its former capital. It is a prominent commercial center, celebrated for its unique silver filigree work. The city is situated between the Mahanadi and Kathajodi rivers, enhancing its scenic beauty.

Cuttack Chandi Temple

The Cuttack Chandi Temple is an ancient temple dedicated to the Goddess Chandi, the presiding deity of Cuttack, Odisha. The temple is located nearby the banks of the Mahanadi River.

Time to Explore: 20 mins

Odisha State Maritime Museum

Located at the historic Jobra Workshop, the museum offers insights into Odisha's maritime history with ten galleries showcasing artifacts like ancient navigation systems and replicas of boats.

Time to Explore: 90 mins

Closed on: Monday

Entry Fee: ₹10 per person

Dhabaleswar Temple

Perched on an island in the Mahanadi River, this temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It's renowned for its serene ambiance and intricate stone carvings dating back to the 10th and 11th centuries.

Time to Explore: 120 mins

Closed on: Open on all days

Entry Fee: No Entry Fee

Peacock Valley

Situated near Naraj Dam, this serene valley is home to numerous peacocks and offers a tranquil environment for nature lovers and photographers. It's an ideal spot for picnics and bird-watching.

Time to Explore: 30 mins

Closed on: Open on all days

Entry Fee: No Entry Fee
